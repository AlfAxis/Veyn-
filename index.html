Home›The Lore
Chapter I

The Origin of the Sovereign

Long before the realm was named, there existed only shadow and the cold breath of the northern seas. From these depths emerged a figure neither bound by time nor restrained by oath — Cealis Veyne, the Shadow Sovereign. Born not of royalty, but of will. Forged not in fire, but in silence.

His left arm, lost in the war of the Silent Passage, was replaced not with flesh but with precision — a mechanical limb of unknown origin, said to be stronger than any blade and colder than the deepest trench. He wears no crown. He wears a mask. The mask is the crown.

Chapter II

The Razorbill Warden

In the northern seas swims a bird that never sleeps. The razorbill does not sing. It does not call out. It watches. When Cealis Veyne emerged from the depths after the Silent Passage, a single razorbill followed him ashore and has not left since.

The Warden — as it came to be known — bears a crimson core embedded in its chest, a prosthetic leg of sovereign engineering, and a cloak woven with the emblem of the realm. It is a fragment of the Sovereign himself. A witness. A guardian. A friend.

Chapter III

The Mask and the Code

The mask was not designed to hide. It was designed to reveal — but only to those worthy of seeing. Its luminous lines shift with the Sovereign's state of mind: white for calm, gold for purpose, crimson for wrath. Those who learn to read the mask understand Cealis Veyne more deeply than any words could allow.

His code: Loyalty is not given. It is proven. His pursuit: not power, but its rightful claim. The hunt continues. The shadow walks. And the warden watches.

The shadow walks.

— Cealis Veyne, Order // 001
